I started journaling to be more intentional and mindful — to actually notice the shifts happening instead of letting the days blur together. A lot has been changing lately, most of it quietly and most of it good, and I wanted somewhere to make sense of it.

## Starting with the basics

The foundation, for me, is health. I'll be honest that my sleep and habits still have a long way to go — screen time before bed and right when I wake up are the first things on the list. I've also felt distant from God, and I want to be honest with myself about that rather than pretend I'm not.

Even so, I've been off a few bad habits for the last couple of weeks, and I can already feel it in my overall wellbeing. It makes me wonder why I didn't start sooner. I think I was just so unfulfilled in other parts of my life that I never had the footing to.

## A change in environment

What's different now is the environment, and how grateful I am for it. The opportunities in front of me are genuinely the ones I want, in the space I want to be in. My confidence has shifted, and I feel more mentally and emotionally stable — not letting outside factors consume me as much.

So I want to keep growing and building the skills to go far. Creating things has been amazing lately, and I don't want to stop. The gym has been great; cooking and my diet have been solid. A lot of things are falling into place, and all glory goes to God for this life. It only feels right to start my days reading a passage, journaling a bit, and spending time in prayer.

When I look ahead, my goals are simple: build a real relationship with Christ and actually understand it instead of going through the motions, keep growing each day by building and pushing my hardest, stay consistent with the gym and my health, and take care of my ankle.

## The part that took the longest

Relationally, I feel like I'm coming back to life. My strengths are being aware and funny while staying uplifting and real — but I've kept a filter up for a while, after a long run of ups and downs. When those waves first hit, I felt lost. I spent so much time trying to understand what was wrong and how to manage it, but I didn't fully know where the feelings were coming from or what they were pointing me toward. I kept riding the highs and lows, trying to make sense of them, without ever reaching the root.

I'd spend hours at night reflecting, trying to work through it, but I kept looking for the answer in the wrong places. I thought that if I could just excel at the standards ingrained in me, fulfillment would finally come — that once I reached some point, everything would feel whole. It never did. Even when I pushed for those things, the feeling never really came.

## Distractions, and the gap they create

Over time, chasing the wrong things only pulled me further from who I really was. The more I searched for fulfillment in the wrong places, the more disconnected I became from the driven version of me — the one who cared deeply, created freely, and pushed hard.

This is why distractions are so important to address: even one or two things weighing you down can quietly pull you from 80% to 50%. That difference compounds day after day, and over time it becomes the gap between feeling aligned and feeling like you're slowly drifting from yourself.

Cutting those distractions off and actually focusing within has helped me see that a lot of the void came from misdirected energy and a lack of action toward a fulfilling purpose. I've spent time alone before and reflected before, but this time feels different — because I'm not just sitting with my thoughts. I'm putting my energy toward what I truly want and working toward it with everything I have. That's what created the shift.

## Coming back

Now I feel much stronger — not because everything is perfect, but because I'm finally coming back to my true self. I want to drop the filter so I can let my good traits show again. I'm still focusing on building and taking care of myself, something I let slide for years. Working toward a purpose and getting more aligned with who I really am is teaching me to love myself more, and it feels like I'm finally tending a garden inside myself. I want to keep at it.

I'm reconnecting with the kid in me who once had big aspirations and nothing in his way. This is just the start of a journey I want to stay on for a long time — and I want to keep building it with God at the center.
