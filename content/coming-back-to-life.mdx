## Where the shift came from

Cutting off distractions and really focusing within has helped me realize that a lot of the void I experienced came from misdirected energy and a lack of action toward a fulfilling purpose. I've spent time alone before and reflected before, but this time feels different because I'm not just sitting with my thoughts. I'm actually putting my energy toward what I truly want and working toward it with everything I have. That's what has helped create this shift in me.

Distractions are extremely important to address because even one or two things weighing you down can quietly pull you from 80% to 50%. That difference compounds day after day, and over time it becomes the gap between feeling aligned and feeling like you're slowly drifting from yourself.

## Looking in the wrong places

Relationally, I feel like I'm coming back to life. I've kept a filter on things for some time now after going through numerous ups and downs. When those waves first hit me, I felt lost. I spent so much time trying to understand what was wrong and how to manage it, but I didn't fully know where those feelings were coming from or what they were pointing me toward. I kept riding the highs and lows, trying to make sense of them, but not fully understanding the root.

I think that's where I started plummeting in the first place. I would spend hours at night reflecting, trying to figure out what was wrong and how to work through it. But I think I kept looking for the answer in the wrong places. I thought that if I could just excel at the standards that had been ingrained into me, then true fulfillment would finally come. I kept believing that once I reached that point, everything would feel whole. But even when I pushed for those things, that feeling never really came.

Over time, that only pulled me further away from who I really was. The more I searched for fulfillment in the wrong places, the more disconnected I became from the driven version of me that cared deeply, created freely, and pushed hard toward things. I feel like that's when things started going downhill in a lot of areas of my life too.

A big part of it was learning to put myself first and stop dimming my light just to make other people feel better. Honestly, that might have been my biggest weakness. Because of society and everything around me, I genuinely dimmed myself for a long time, and I let my drive get more and more shallow. I was a people pleaser, plain and simple. A lot of my friends are amazing, so this was never really about them. It was just a personal change I had to make for myself. But I realized I have this God-given life, and I want to make the most of it, not stay stuck living as a shadow.

## Health and faith

A big thing for me is my health. My sleep and health habits can still be a lot better. Screen time before bed and after I wake up are things I can improve on. I'm distant from God, and I want to be honest with myself about that. However, I'm happy to say that I've been off some bad habits for the last couple of weeks, and I can without a doubt say I'm already feeling shifts in my overall wellbeing. It makes me think, like, why didn't I make these changes before? I guess I was just so unfulfilled in other aspects of my life.

I'm extremely grateful for the opportunities that I have right now, and it's really the environment that I want to be in. I can feel a shift in my confidence, and I feel more mentally and emotionally stable, not letting outside factors consume me as much.

I want to continue growing every day and really build the skills to go far in life. Creating things has been amazing lately, and I don't ever want to stop. The gym has been great. Cooking and my diet have been pretty good too. I think a lot of things are falling into place, and all glory goes to God for this life. It only feels right to start my days reading a passage, journaling a bit, and spending time in prayer.

Reflection is such a beautiful thing, and it always calms me to stop for a bit and take a moment to gather everything that has been happening recently. Looking ahead, what are my goals? I definitely think it's to form a better relationship with Christ and actually understand more instead of just going through the motions. I also want to continue growing each day by building, pushing my hardest, staying consistent with the gym and health, and taking care of my ankle as well so I can return to playing sports at a high level. It brings me so much competitive joy and balance with my rigorous work life.

## Coming back

Now I feel much stronger, not because everything is perfect, but because I feel like I'm finally coming back to my true self again. I want to remove that filter so I can let my positive traits shine to those around me again. I think all of this is coming back with me focusing on building and taking care of myself, something I've let slide for many of my past years. Working toward a fulfilling purpose and becoming more aligned with who I truly am is teaching me to love myself more. It feels like I'm finally creating that garden within myself, and I want to continue that.

I'm finally reconnecting to what seems like the kid in me who once had all these big aspirations and nothing stopping me. This is just the start of a journey I want to stay on for a long time, and I want to keep building it with God at the center.
