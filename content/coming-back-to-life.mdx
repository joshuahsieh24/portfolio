---
title: "Coming back to life"
publishedAt: "2026-06-23"
summary: "Starting to journal to be more intentional and mindful — getting healthier, getting closer to God, dropping old habits, and reconnecting with the kid in me who had big dreams and nothing in the way."
---

I want to journal more to be intentional and mindful.

A big thing for me is my health. My sleep and health habits can still be a lot better. Screen time before bed and after I wake up are things I can improve on. I'm distant from God, and I want to be honest with myself about that. However, I'm happy to say that I've been off some bad habits for the last couple of weeks, and I can without a doubt say I'm already feeling shifts in my overall wellbeing. It makes me think, like, why didn't I make these changes before? I guess I was just so unfulfilled in other aspects of my life.

I'm extremely grateful for the opportunities that I have right now, and it's really the environment that I want to be in. I can feel a shift in my confidence, and I feel more mentally and emotionally stable, not letting outside factors consume me as much.

I want to continue growing every day and really build the skills to go far in life. Creating things has been amazing lately, and I don't ever want to stop. The gym has been great. Cooking and my diet have been pretty good too. I think a lot of things are falling into place, and all glory should be toward God for this life. It only feels right to start my days reading a passage, journaling a bit, and spending time in prayer.

Reflection is such a beautiful thing, and it always calms me to stop for a bit and take a moment to gather everything that has been happening recently. Looking ahead, what are my goals? I definitely think it's to form a better relationship with Christ and actually understand more instead of just going through the motions. I also want to continue growing each day by building, pushing my hardest, staying consistent with the gym and health, and taking care of my ankle as well.

Relationally, I feel like I'm coming back to life. I think my strengths are being aware and funny while also being able to be uplifting and real. I've kept a filter on things for some time now after going through numerous ups and downs. When those waves first hit me, I felt lost. I didn't know what was wrong or how to manage it, so I just kept riding the highs and lows without really understanding where they were coming from.

I think that's where I started plummeting in the first place. I did try to sit with what I was feeling and understand it. I would spend hours at night reflecting, trying to figure out what was wrong and how to work through it. But I think I kept looking for the answer in the wrong places. I thought that if I could just excel at the standards that had been ingrained into me, then true fulfillment would finally come. I kept believing that once I reached that point, everything would feel whole. But even when I pushed for those things, that feeling never really came.

Over time, that only pulled me further away from who I really was. The more I searched outside of myself for fulfillment, the more disconnected I became from the driven version of me that cared deeply, created freely, and pushed hard toward things. I feel like that's when things started going downhill in a lot of areas of my life too.

Spending time alone has helped me realize that the void I experienced was really a result of misdirected energy and a lack of action toward a fulfilling purpose. Now I feel much stronger, not because everything is perfect, but because I feel like I'm finally coming back to my true self again.

I want to remove that filter in a way where I can shine my positive traits to those around me again. I think all of this is coming back with me focusing on building and taking care of myself, something I've let slide for many of my past years. I'm not sure what is teaching me to love myself more, but it's really creating that garden, and I want to continue that.

I'm finally reconnecting to what seems like the kid in me who once had all these big aspirations and nothing stopping me. This is just the start of a journey I want to ride for a long time, and I want to keep building it with God at the center.
